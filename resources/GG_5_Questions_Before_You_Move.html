1
Positioning
Where exactly is this equipment going, and is that spot ready?

Unclear destinations cause last-minute re-routes, rushed decisions, and near-misses. Define the endpoint before you start.

  • Final destination is confirmed and communicated to all crew members
  • Landing zone is clear, level, and capable of supporting the load
  • Path from here to there is free of obstructions (overhead lines, soft ground, tight turns)
Additional notes / destination details
2
Roles
Does everyone know their specific job during this move?

Assumed roles lead to doubled-up or missing tasks. Name who is doing what — out loud — before the equipment moves.

  • One person is designated as the signal person / spotter
  • Driver/operator has acknowledged and accepted their role
  • Ground crew knows where to stand and where NOT to stand
Role assignments
3
Sequence
What is the step-by-step order of this move?

Complex moves have multiple stages. Identify the sequence before starting so no one improvises mid-move when stakes are highest.

  • Load/attachment sequence is agreed on before lift or pull begins
  • Any staging or hold points during the move are identified
  • Crew knows the stop/abort signal if conditions change
Move sequence / key steps
4
Communication
How will crew communicate during the move — and what does STOP mean?

In a loud, fast-moving environment, mixed signals are dangerous. Set the signals and the stop protocol before anything moves.

  • Hand signals or radio channel confirmed with all crew
  • Any crew member has authority to call a full stop
  • Stop signal is clearly understood by operator and all spotters
Signal method and STOP signal
5
Hazards
What could go wrong on this specific move — and what's the plan?

Generic safety talks don't prevent specific accidents. Identify the one or two real risks on this move and name a concrete response.

  • At least one specific hazard for this move has been named
  • A response or mitigation for that hazard is agreed on
  • Crew knows what to do if someone is struck or trapped
