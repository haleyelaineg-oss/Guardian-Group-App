01 Equipment & Trailer Match
  • Trailer/vehicle is rated for the load weight and type
  • Trailer floor and walls are in good condition — no cracks, holes, or weak points
  • Tie-down anchors and D-rings are intact and free of damage
  • Correct securement equipment is on hand (chains, straps, binders, blocks)
  • Working Load Limit (WLL) of each device meets or exceeds requirements for load
Notes
02 Loading Position & Surface
  • Load is centered and distributed evenly across axles
  • Heaviest items are placed lowest and toward the front of trailer
  • No load overhangs beyond legal limits without proper flagging/lighting
  • Load does not obstruct mirrors, lights, or license plate
  • Trailer deck/bed is clean and free of grease, ice, or debris that could shift load
Notes
03 Securement
  • Minimum number of tie-downs applied per FMCSA 49 CFR §393.106
  • All straps and chains are properly seated with no twists or knots
  • All binders are locked and will not release under vibration
  • Edge protectors used where tie-downs contact sharp edges
  • Blocking, bracing, or chocking used where required to prevent forward movement
  • Loose items or dunnage secured separately — nothing left to shift freely
Notes
04 In-Transit Responsibilities
  • Load will be re-inspected within 50 miles of departure (or as required)
  • Load will be re-inspected after any change in load condition
  • Crew knows route restrictions (height, weight, permit requirements)
  • Escort vehicle arranged if required for oversized/overweight load
  • Emergency equipment (flags, triangles, fire extinguisher) is accessible and serviceable
Notes
05 Before Unloading
  • Delivery/unloading area is clear and stable
  • Any load shift during transit is documented and communicated to receiver
  • All tie-downs are released in safe sequence — no binders under tension released first
  • Unloading equipment (forklift, crane, etc.) is rated and ready
  • Personnel are clear of load path during unloading
