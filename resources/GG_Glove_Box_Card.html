How to fill out this card

Truck GVWR & towing capacity: Look inside the driver-side door jamb for the manufacturer's sticker. GVWR is listed there. Max towing capacity is in the owner's manual or on the same sticker.

Trailer GVWR & empty weight: Both are on a metal plate riveted to the trailer tongue or frame. GVWR is the max the trailer can hold. Empty weight is the trailer itself — these are two different numbers.

Equipment actual weight: Check the manufacturer's data plate on the machine, or the operator's manual. If unknown, weigh it on a truck scale. Estimated ranges are not sufficient for compliance — get the actual number.

Tiedown requirements: Under 10,000 lbs equipment = minimum 2 tiedowns. Over 10,000 lbs = minimum 4 tiedowns, each rated at 5,000 lb WLL minimum (49 CFR §393.130).

Laminate this card and keep it in the glove box or sun visor slot. Update it when equipment or trailer assignments change.
